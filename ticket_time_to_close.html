How to read this dashboard

What is "time to close"?

The number of calendar days between when a ticket was created and when it was marked closed in HubSpot. This includes weekends and any time a ticket was on hold — it's wall-clock time, not active working time.

Why median instead of average?

Many tickets are created in one month but don't get resolved for weeks. When they finally close, they pull the average up dramatically — e.g. a ticket open for 80 days inflates the mean for its closing month. The median (the middle value when all tickets are sorted) is not affected by these outliers and better represents the typical customer experience.

What does "n" mean?

n is the count of tickets in that category that were closed during that month. It is not the number of tickets opened. For example, if 209 HW tickets closed in June, n=209 — some of those may have been opened months earlier.

How are categories defined?

HubSpot tickets have a primary_category field. Robot HW = ROBOT_HAREDWARE, Robot SW = ROBOT_SOFTWARE, App = APP. All other values — including Shipping, Returns, Order Mgmt, Duplicates, and blank entries — are grouped as Others.

Data coverage

All tickets closed between Jan 1 – Jun 15, 2026 were fetched from HubSpot (87–99% of each month's total). Monthly totals shown are the exact HubSpot counts. A small final page per month (~1–13%) was not captured, so n values may be slightly understated.

Color coding in the table

Green = ≤ 1 day  ·  White = 1–6 days  ·  Yellow = 6–10 days  ·  Red = > 10 days. Thresholds are approximate — use as a directional guide, not hard SLAs.
