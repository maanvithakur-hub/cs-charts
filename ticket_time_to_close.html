How to read this dashboard

What is being measured?

Business hours between when a ticket was created and when it was closed in HubSpot. Only Mon–Fri, 10am–7pm PT counts — nights and weekends are excluded. For HW tickets specifically: tickets are manually closed when the return shipping label is sent — so this measures time to triage and initiate the return, not the full repair cycle. For SW, App, and Others, "closed" reflects full resolution.

Why filter to tickets ≤ 30 days old?

Many tickets sit open for months before finally being closed — a ticket created in October 2025 and closed in January 2026 would show up as a January ticket with a massive time-to-close, skewing the entire month. Limiting to tickets resolved within 30 days removes this backlog drag and shows the current team's actual resolution speed.

Why median instead of average?

Even after the 30-day filter, a few tickets take much longer than typical. The median (the middle value when all tickets are sorted by duration) ignores these outliers and represents what a typical customer experiences. The mean would still be pulled up by the slowest 5–10% of tickets.

What does "n" mean?

n is the count of tickets in that category that were closed in that month and resolved within 30 days of creation. Tickets older than 30 days at close are excluded from both the chart and the n count.

How are categories defined?

HubSpot tickets have a primary_category field. Robot HW = ROBOT_HAREDWARE, Robot SW = ROBOT_SOFTWARE, App = APP. All other values — Shipping, Returns, Order Mgmt, Duplicates, blank entries — are grouped as Others.

Color coding in the table

Green = ≤ 9h (same day)  ·  White = 9–45h (1–5 biz days)  ·  Yellow = 45–72h (5–8 biz days)  ·  Red = > 72h (> 8 biz days).
